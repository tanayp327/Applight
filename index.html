About NICOA

The National Indian Council on Aging(NICOA) was founded in 1976 by members of the National Tribal Chairmen's Association who called for a national organization focused on American Indian and Alaska Native elders. They wanted an organization with the mission of advocating for improved comprehensive health, sovial services, and economic well-being of American Indian and Alaska Native elders. They provide testimony in court for AI and AN elders as well as participate in the Senior Community Service Employment Program (SCSEP) to provide on-the-job training and employment programs to help low income AI and AN elders throughout 7 different states and they have served over 300,000 elders at their conferences.They also partner with the Alzheimer's Association to provide 570 tribes Alzheimer's awareness as well as care and support resources. NICOA is partnered with the American Society on Aging to present at conferences and bring awareness to the specific needs of AI and AN elders.

Testimonials

NICOA advocates for American Indian and Alaska Native elders, mainly focusing on their economic well being with NICOA's SCSEP program. AI/AN elders are disproportionatly low in nearly every social, health and economic factor with the highest poverty rates at 24.1%, while the national average is 12.8% and AI/AN elders are also 50.3% more likely to have a disablity NICOA serves AI/AN elders in 7 states with their SCSEP program: Arizona, Minnesota, New Mexico, North Dakota, South Dakota, Oklahoma, and Wisconsin.
